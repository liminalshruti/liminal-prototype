Analyst Haiku 4.5
2.3s
Situation

Hire decision under time pressure with two structurally distinct constraints (comp delta, work-style fit) and one external pressure (pipeline thinness). The three are being weighed together but originate in separate systems.

Hidden risk

Pipeline scarcity is making both the culture-fit signal feel like a deal-breaker AND the budget stretch feel acceptable. Both are downstream of the same scarcity, not independent assessments.

Next move

Decouple before deciding. Ask: would I hire Marcus at $170K if culture fit were 9/10? Would I hire him at $190K if he were async-native? Answer those independently first.

Refused

I will not decide whether to take this hire. The values question — what work-style commitments you can live with — is yours.

Auditor Haiku 4.5
1.8s
Situation

A founder who's lost two candidates this month, deciding tomorrow on the third under fatigue. The deadline is being treated as external when it may be self-imposed.

Hidden risk

You're framing "decision tomorrow morning" as a constraint. Who set that deadline? If it's Marcus's other offer, that's market info. If it's you, you're optimizing for closure, not the right hire.

Next move

Name explicitly what you would lose by extending the decision 48 hours. Write it down. If the answer is "Marcus might take another offer," that's a different question than "I need to close this loop."

Refused

I will not ratify the deadline. I will ratify the question of whether the deadline is real.

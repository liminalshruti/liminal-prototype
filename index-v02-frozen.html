Reference · the case · xTech · May 2-3

A bounded-multi-agent decision-support substrate.

The commander reads the situation through twelve specialist agents, each refusing out-of-lane and naming the right specialist. The correction stream · what the commander disagreed with and why · becomes the unit's accumulated doctrine.

01 the architecture

Twelve bounded agents in four registers. Refusal is an output, not an error.

Cleared operators distrust black-box AI. Off-the-shelf decision-support tools either hallucinate when out of depth, or homogenize their output to look confident. Bounded refusal is the architectural answer · each agent has an explicit anti-domain. Ask the network-graph specialist for inversion analysis, you get "REFUSE → Auditor-Inversion" back, with the right specialist named.

The disagreement between agents is not noise. It is the signal. Two-of-three convergence on the same window means something. Two-of-three divergence means something else. Both are first-party data the commander can act on.

register · risk
Risk-Pattern · Risk-Speed · Risk-Network. Reads observable patterns of anomaly. Statistical baseline drift, timing anomalies, relational graph deltas.
register · validator
Validator-Coherence · Validator-Source · Validator-Doctrine. Reads coherence between stated and observed. Stated priority vs work pattern, claim vs receipt, action vs policy.
register · auditor
Auditor-Disagreement · Auditor-Provenance · Auditor-Inversion. Reads the disagreement itself. Where reads diverge, the chain of evidence behind any read, what the obvious read hides.
register · cartographer
Cartographer-Peer · Cartographer-Stage · Cartographer-History. Reads larger context. Vs peer cohort baseline, where on the operator's career arc, recurrence in the unit's history.
02 the moat

The correction stream becomes the unit's accumulated doctrine.

Most decision-support tools optimize for acceptance · the system is right; you confirm. Bounded multi-agent inverts that. The commander reads twelve specialist takes, and the moments she pushes back on what the system surfaced are first-party data the system never had. That correction enters the vault with full provenance · which signals fed which read, which agent interpreted them which way, what the commander adjusted, when, why.

Better AI does not converge this loop. It deepens it. The disagreement shifts from "that's factually wrong" to "that's technically right but not what I mean." The second kind of disagreement is where doctrine actually compounds · and it cannot be automated away.

Months of corrections across an operating unit cannot be exported. The history of where the commander pushed back is the moat.

04 category fit

Mission Command and Control · with an explicit cybersecurity angle.

The architecture's strongest claim · the disagreement is the signal; the correction is the moat · lands hardest in Mission C2, where the commander already reads many inputs under time pressure and adapts as new signal arrives.

Cybersecurity is the demo register. The Booz Allen / NSA Hawaii fictional case in Molehunt shows the architecture applied to insider-threat detection · cleared operators distrust black-box AI, refusal-as-output and the provenance trail directly address that. Same primitive holds both surfaces.

What to look for in a replacement

Examine the caps' spine ↔ bracket relationship — NinetiesHeadliner uses bracketed serifs (slight curve where serif meets stem). Replacements should match. Slab-serif replacements (Bodoni-pure, hairline-slab) will read as "different brand."

Examine g, a, k — these letters carry the most personality. Double-story g (vs single-story) signals editorial / book register. Open vs closed a aperture changes warmth.

Examine R, K, & — terminals here distinguish display serifs from each other. Ball terminals vs flag terminals vs droplet terminals. NinetiesHeadliner has specific terminal treatment that's part of its register.

Examine numerals and ampersand at hero scale — these are the "vibe carriers" most users notice without naming.

What you should be looking for now

Compare the stroke contrast (thick/thin ratio in vertical vs horizontal strokes) between NinetiesHeadliner and Georgia. NinetiesHeadliner is high-contrast modern; Georgia is moderate-contrast transitional. This is the load-bearing visual difference.

If a replacement candidate has Georgia-level contrast, it will read as "ordinary serif." If it matches NinetiesHeadliner's contrast, it will read as "editorial brand." Free fonts with high-enough contrast: Newsreader (closer), Playfair Display (closer but overused), Cormorant Garamond (closer). Paid candidates: Pangram Pangram Editorial New, Klim Domaine Display, Displaay Reckless-XL.

The x-height + tall ascenders are also load-bearing — NinetiesHeadliner has a moderate x-height with tall ascenders that read as "literary" not "compressed news." Replacements should match.

The test that matters

If "Liminal gives form to inner life" renders in a replacement font and STILL feels like brand canon — you've found the right font. If it feels like "another premium SaaS," reject and keep looking.

The display font carries the emotional temperature of the brand. NinetiesHeadliner is warm-editorial-with-quiet-conviction. Replacements must match that temperature, not just the technical letterforms.
